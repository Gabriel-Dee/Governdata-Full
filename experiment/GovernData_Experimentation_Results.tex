\begin{abstract}

\end{abstract}

\section{Experiment 1 \& 6: Latency and Engine Comparison}
Client-side timings: \texttt{experiment/scripts/full\_experiment\_run.py}, 8 iterations $\times$ 3 scenarios, 2 warmup rounds discarded. \textbf{Note}: \texttt{/api/v1/benchmark/policy-runtime} is invoked with \texttt{iterations=1} because the server rejects duplicate \texttt{requestId} across benchmark iterations (HTTP 409).

\begin{longtable}{p{4.5cm}p{2cm}p{2cm}p{2cm}p{2.5cm}}
\toprule
Run artifact (label) & Avg (ms) & Min (ms) & Max (ms) & p95 (ms) \\
\midrule
\texttt{JSON\_BOTH} & 2085.11 & 2040.88 & 2353.16 & 2125.88 \\
\texttt{OPA\_BOTH\_MATRIX} & 2081.61 & 2041.50 & 2320.15 & 2106.08 \\
\texttt{OPA\_DB\_ONLY} & 19.73 & 11.57 & 36.44 & 29.16 \\
\texttt{OPA\_BLOCKCHAIN\_ONLY} & 2066.38 & 2024.22 & 2132.47 & 2108.67 \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

\textbf{Interpretation}: Authorize latency with \texttt{BOTH} and real Fabric includes ledger commit time ($\sim$2\,s locally). \texttt{DB\_ONLY} configuration dramatically reduces measured authorize latency in this setup because the Fabric anchor path is not used for those measurements in the same way --- compare only within the same storage mode when reporting thesis numbers.
